About me

My name’s Robin and I’m a web designer from the south west of England. Now I live in cloudy San Francisco where I work as a product designer at Gusto. My day job is spent building our design system, contributing to the foundations of our front-end codebase and working on the underlying UX of the application.

I also write, edit and publish things alongside the team at CSS-Tricks. Although most of my time is now spent on the newsletter where we collect everything that happened in this little community over the course of any given week. So if you’re interested in front-end development then I’d highly recommend you sign up.

Partly inspired by Chris’ and Zach’s timelines, here’s a list of events that were really important for me and my career in web design:

Timeline

10/2008
Started my undergrad degree at Plymouth University in English Literature.
I almost flunked out of university because I spent so much time writing music and then at the last moment I was obsessed with writing a ten thousand word epic poem about Bethlehem asylum.
10/2011
Studied an MA in Information Design at the prestigious and scary Department of Typography and Graphic Communication at Reading University.
Kinda had my ego thrown into the heart of the nearest star when I suddenly discovered that design is more than nice fonts and pretty colors.
02/2012
Worked with the writer/editor Alex Charchar on my first piece for Smashing Magazine.
It was a long and grueling process because I was learning how to write and design at the same time. I remember one moment where Alex was trying desperately to teach me about contrast in photography and it just wouldn’t sink in.
11/2012
Moved to Nottingham in the UK to start working with the team at Erskine as a front-end developer. Figured out the command line and CSS.
06/2014
I left Erskine and started freelancing as a front-end developer and designer (I moved into my mom’s loft).
12/2014
Joined the writing team at CSS-Tricks and began writing regularly for the website, covering all sorts of front-end development and design topics.
06/2016
Navigated the American visa process and moved to San Francisco to join the team at Gusto as a product designer and UI engineer.
